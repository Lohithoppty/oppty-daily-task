The Nissan Skyline GT-R R34 (produced 1999-2002) is a legendary Japanese sports car, famous for its high-tech performance, distinctive looks, and iconic status in movies and games, powered by the legendary RB26 twin-turbo inline-six engine and featuring advanced ATTESA E-TS all-wheel drive and Super-HICAS four-wheel steering. Known as "Godzilla Returns," it's a highly sought-after JDM (Japanese Domestic Market) icon, with increasing value due to its limited availability and 25-year import eligibility in the US.

The Toyota Supra MK4 (A80), produced from 1993-2002, is an iconic Japanese sports car famous for its powerful 2JZ engine, sleek design, and starring role in The Fast and the Furious, making it a highly sought-after collector's item, known for its robust twin-turbo inline-six (330 hp) and available with a 6-speed manual transmission, though its interior reflects 90s tech compared to modern GTs.

The Mazda RX-7 FD (third generation, 1992-2002) is a legendary lightweight Japanese sports car famous for its stunning design, exceptional handling, and unique twin-turbo 1.3L 13B rotary engine, offering a visceral, near-perfect 50:50 weight distribution and a smooth, powerful drive thanks to its sequential turbos. It's praised as a driver's car with precise steering and balanced chassis, despite its rotary engine's specific maintenance needs, making it a sought-after collector's item.
